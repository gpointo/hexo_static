Disciplines The Key

Sep 23, 2017

Starting is hard

Discipline is hard to keep up with in the beginning. It takes discipline to overcome your hardest struggles whether it be getting over an ex, quiting smoking, getting in shape, being better at your craft, understanding your passion, or simply getting a grip on life’. I’m writing this blog to become a better person. In many of the ‘join the army’ propaganda they always ask “if your life was a book would anyone want to read it?”. I think mine would be an interesting and morally challenging book. But I want to change the direction of my life and that book.



Let me start out by introducing myself to all those reading this post, as most of you don’t know me. My name is Giovanni Ortiz. I have a hard time keeping track of time, and try to make up with lack of sleep. I workout occasionally, I really like it and I hope this blog will keep me motivated to stick with my goals such as this one. The times in my life when I was disciplined enough to stop my vices from taking over I was able to be the best version of me… I was able to forget the the feeling of being alone. And able to focus on what I needed to do to get ahead. But, now at the ripe age of of 25 I find myself in the same hole I always get myself in.



There is one thing that I know… That is that when I have a schedule, when I have a plan; I may get lonely, I may feel the weight of a regimen to keep up, but I’m doing good in life. When I’m able to fight off my urges to spend money, when I hold true to my principals, and I keep a tight regimen, my finances move towards there goals, my health is good, I do good in my job and in fact excel at everything I do… and I want to be that… I want to be in good standing to support myself again like I once was… only this time when I get there again I want to setup the relationships needed to keep me from getting depressed and to be able to relate to people and not distance myself…



In conclusion, I hope that my own struggle helps anyone in theirs and inspires them to be better, day by day. I’m writing about change, commitment, and the things it takes to be the best version of yourself. I hope you guys enjoy my blog and leave comments tips, anything really!



And on that note I would like for my readers to read this following quote by Clint Eastwood and reflect on it during the week cause I know I will…

“Respect your efforts, respect yourself. Self-respect leads to self-discipline. When you have both firmly under your belt, that’s real power.”
